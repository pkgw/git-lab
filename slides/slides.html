Radio studies might give a new handle on magnetic topologies in the ultracool regime.

The powerful Zeeman-Doppler imaging (ZDI) technique has suggested a bimodality in ultracool field topologies.

Vidotto+ 2014

Morin+ 2010

But ZDI is very difficult to apply ≳M5, and is insensitive to blended antiparallel fields.

Radio data provide vital, complementary insight for the coolest objects.

The inferred rotation rate matches known trends.

Fin.

  • We’ve used the VLA to measure the fifth-ever rotation period for a late T dwarf, the unique radio emitter 2M 1047+21.
  • The imperfect periodicity motivates a model with transient radio bursts originating from one of several magnetic loops.
  • This example shows how radio data can probe the topology of the magnetic field in regimes where ZDI and other techniques are not feasible.
